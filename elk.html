The Hunt

You'll see more elk than you ever have before

During the fall, thousands of elk inhabit our ranch. You can expect to see hundreds of elk each day during your hunt. Northwest Colorado's elk population grows nearly every year, and Smith Rancho sits in the middle of some of the best country in the state.

The average bull taken here is a strong 5-point, with several 6-points harvested every season. Shots typically range from 50 to 300 yards. As colder temperatures arrive in late October, the elk move more and group up — and you'll see larger herds spilling out of the timber.

Over the years we've maintained a success rate around 98%, with 100% opportunity to harvest an elk. Because we're part of Big Gulch Ranching for Wildlife, your license is guaranteed — no draw, no application, no waiting.

Rut · Sept 1 – Oct 15

When the bulls are bugling

September and early October is when elk hunting gets electric. During the rut, the bulls are bugling and our guides call them in to close range. There's nothing like hearing a herd bull answer a call and come crashing through the timber toward you.

Our guides know this country the way Brad Smith has known it since 1970. They read the wind, work the elk, and put you in position. You bring the patience — we handle the rest.

Late Season · Oct 16 – Nov 29

Cold temps, big herds

As the weather turns in late October and November, elk behavior shifts. They leave the heavy timber and travel in large herds across open terrain — giving you long shooting lanes and unforgettable Rocky Mountain scenery.

For many of our returning hunters, the late season becomes their favorite. With private access to 62,000 acres and a full 90-day window, you can hunt the conditions that suit your style.
